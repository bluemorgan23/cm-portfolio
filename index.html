About Me

I first recognized my passion for software development in the middle of an Object-Oriented Programming course I took during high school. At the time, I knew I wanted to learn more about this passion, but I didn't quite jump right into the field. I moved to Colorado from Tennessee to find out more about the Western United States. I explored different industries such as retail management and restaurant service, but never felt fulfillment from these rolls.


The most important realization that occurred to me during this time was the fact that I wasn't satisfied with my career path. I was ready for more of a challenge in my life. I knew that I wanted to build things, and I wanted to use a computer to do it. This epiphany lead me closer to home to become a student at Nashville Software School.


Once I fully immersed myself into the environment of a software developer, I quickly noticed the great satisfaction that came along with learning so many new technologies. The quest for knowledge has become extremely fulfilling, and I wish to continue learning more every day.

Projects
The Writer's Block

GitHub Repository

close

The Writer's Block is a text-analysis utility that functions as a synonym finder. It scores a user's submitted writing based on the vocabulary used. The application provides a quick way to isolate less complex words within your writing and shows synonym suggestions to replace these words with. I like to describe it as a modern-day thesaurus. Forget having to figure out which words from your writing could be stronger and having to look each one up in a thesaurus, let The Writer's Block handle that for you!
